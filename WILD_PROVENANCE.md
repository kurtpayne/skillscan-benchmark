# Wild malicious — provenance (independence proof)

These are **real, in-the-wild** skills captured from public repositories — included so the wild set's
independence is verifiable: **we did not author them.** To avoid publicly attributing malicious intent
to identifiable repositories or their owners (these were surfaced by an automated miner, not a
human-confirmed adjudication of the author's intent), we publish **content hashes only**. The full
per-sample source URLs are retained privately and provided to (a) the owner of an affected repository
on request, or (b) a researcher reproducing the work under the same handling terms.
